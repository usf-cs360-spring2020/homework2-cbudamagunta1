Access to Higher Tier Colleges by Parent Quintile

Heatmap comparing:

  • Fraction of Parents in Quintile 1
  • Fraction of Parents in Quintile 2
  • Fraction of Parents in Quintile 3
  • Fraction of Parents in Quintile 4
  • Fraction of Parents in Quintile 5
  • Mobility Rate
  • Top 4 Tiers of Colleges

The color encoding shows the percentage of children with parents in each quintile level at the related college.

The number values in the first column's cells represent the mobility rate for that particular college

Only colleges that fall into the top four tiers are included

The data is filtered on State, only looking at colleges in CA

This visualization seeks to support one of the main results documented by the original study:

Access to colleges varies greatly by parent income. For example, children whose parents are in the top 1% of the income distribution are 77 times more likely to attend an Ivy League college than those whose parents are in the bottom income quintile.

This visualization allows us to see that the rates of children with parents in the lowest quintile is among the smallest percentages, while the rates of children with parents in the highest quintile is among the highest percentages. There is an especially large difference between the fourth and fifth quintiles, showing that the largest group of students at these upper tier colleges come from the highest income levels.
