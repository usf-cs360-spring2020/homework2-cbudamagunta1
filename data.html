Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility

The Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility project from Opportunity Insights looks at intergenerational income mobility at every US college. They gathered data from over 30 million college students from 1999 to 2013. Credited to: Raj Chetty, John Friedman, Emmanuel Saez, Nicholas Turner, and Danny Yagan.

The projects consists of 14 different datasets, each focusing on a different area within the overall subject.

Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility

Baseline Cross-Sectional Estimates of Child and Parent Income Distributions by College

These visualizations focus specifically on the “Baseline Cross-Sectional Estimates of Child and Parent Income Distributions by College” dataset, which reports their baseline estimates of parents’ and children’s income distributions by college. The dataset was accessed in February of 2020.

Detailed Column Information

These visualizations focus on certain specific columns:

  • Mobility Rate [mr_kq5_pq1]: joint probablility of parents in bottom quintile and child in top 1% of the income distribution
  • Tier [tier]: selectivity and type combination of colleges, 1 being highest (Ivy Plus) and 14 being lowest (Not in college between ages of 19-22)
  • Mean Kid Earnings [k_mean]
  • Fraction of Parents in Quintile 1-5 [par_q[PARQUINT]]: 1 is the bottom quintile and 5 is the top
  • Mean Kid Earnings Rank Conditional on Parents in Quintile 5 [k_rank_cond_parq5]
  • Mean Kid Earnings Rank Conditional on Parents in Quintile 1 [k_rank_cond_parq1]
  • Median Child Individual Earnings in 2014 [k_median_nozero]

The dataset consists of 36 columns, and holds 2202 records from colleges. As specified in their codebook: "We calculate college-level values as means over students in the 1980, 1981 and 1982 birth cohorts. When data for a college from any of these cohorts are incomplete, we use data from 1983 and 1984 cohorts to obtain an estimate."

The project was last revised December 2017, and all data produced by Opportunity Insights is publicly available to use.
